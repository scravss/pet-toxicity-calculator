💡 Did you know? The classic "multiply by 7" rule is a myth. A dog's first year equals roughly 15 human years — because they mature from puppy to adult very rapidly. The conversion slows significantly after year 2.

How the conversion works

1
Year 1 = 15 human years. Pets mature extremely fast in their first year — reaching full sexual maturity and adult size.
2
Year 2 = +9 human years. The second year adds another major developmental leap — equivalent to a young adult.
3
Each additional year = +4–5 human years (varies by dog size — large dogs age faster). Cats are relatively consistent after year 2.
