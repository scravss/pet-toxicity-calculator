Why Is Chocolate Dangerous for Dogs?

Chocolate contains two methylxanthine compounds: theobromine and, to a lesser extent, caffeine. In humans, these are metabolized quickly and harmlessly. Dogs, however, process theobromine far more slowly — it accumulates in their system and disrupts normal heart, kidney, and nervous system function.

The darker and purer the chocolate, the higher its theobromine content and the greater the risk. Baking chocolate and dark chocolate are the most dangerous; white chocolate contains negligible amounts and poses minimal risk.

Chocolate Type Theobromine (mg/g) Danger Level
Baking / Unsweetened~16 – 26 mg/gVery High
Dark (70%+)~16 mg/gHigh

White Chocolate<0.1 mg/gVery Low
⚠️ Veterinary toxic threshold: symptoms typically begin above 20 mg of theobromine per kg of body weight. Severe effects appear above 40 mg/kg.

What Are the Symptoms of Chocolate Toxicity?

Symptoms usually appear within 6 to 12 hours of ingestion and can last up to 72 hours. Watch for:

  • Vomiting and/or diarrhea (often the first signs)
  • Excessive thirst and frequent urination
  • Restlessness, pacing, and hyperactivity
  • Muscle tremors or twitching
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat (tachycardia)
  • Seizures (in severe cases)
  • Collapse or coma (life-threatening doses)
🚨 If your dog is seizuring, has collapsed, or has an irregular heartbeat — go to an emergency vet immediately. Do not wait.

Immediate Emergency Steps to Take

If you believe your dog has eaten chocolate, act quickly and calmly:

  1. Identify the type and amount. Check packaging, estimate the grams consumed. This information is critical for the vet.
  2. Note the time of ingestion. The sooner you act, the more options your vet has (including inducing vomiting if recent).
  3. Call your vet or a poison helpline immediately. US: ASPCA Poison Control +1-888-426-4435 | UK: Animal PoisonLine 01202 509000.
  4. Do NOT induce vomiting unless explicitly instructed by a veterinary professional.
  5. Use our calculator to quantify the risk level — then share the result with your vet to prioritize treatment.
  6. Monitor closely. Even if symptoms seem mild, keep your dog calm, hydrated, and under observation for at least 12 hours.
